## 8/03/2026 Initial Plan, Goals and BOM

Today is the day I start my project, so there are a couple of things I need to get sorted out. 

**Number 1: What will I need to achieve for a successful robotic arm?**

* 360 degrees of freedom
* 6-axis of movement
* Fully 3D printable except for necessary mechanical and electrical components
* Some form of PCB (Not a necessity, but I would like to advance my skills in making PCBs)
* The ability to stand alone with rechargeability and Bluetooth

**Number 2: Which software will I be using?**

* Onshape will be used for my main CAD software; it may not be ideal, but it is what I am most familiar with and what I have the most access to resources for.
* Kicad is the standard for hobbyist PCB design, and it is also my most familiar software, so I will be utilising that for my electronics.
* The microcontroller will be controlled through Arduino IDE.
  
**Number 3: Chosen electronics, BOM and Budget**

My budget for this project (as a tier two project) is only 200 dollars, so this is gonna be quite tight. 

* ESP32 as the brain (I'm so excited, I've never used the ESP32 before).
* NEMA 17 Stepper motors x6 (most standard pick for CNCing or larger hobbyist robotic arm).
* Stepper drivers x6.
* Power supply.
* Miscellaneous (wires, pcb)
